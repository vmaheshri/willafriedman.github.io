Econ 7395: Social Economics

Instructor: Vikram Maheshri, vmaheshri@uh.edu, McElhinney 221A

Course Overview

This course will survey the growing empirical and theoretical literature on social economics. The topics of study, broadly defined, will be:

  1. Social Learning/Norms/Conformity
  2. Peer Effects and Identification
  3. Sorting (Endogenous Peer Groups)
  4. Identity

We will study a number of theoretical models of social phenomena and then pay particular attention to the empirical implications of these models. We will focus on how social interactions between agents confounds causal inference and explore different strategies that researchers have used to circumvent these issues. We will also consider the implications of the structure of peer groups (networks) and how these arise.

Grading

At this point in your studies, you are supposed to be focused on research, so this course will enrich your understanding of others’ research and will give you an opportunity to develop some of your own. To that end, there will be no exams or problem sets.

Each student will be required to present 3 well known and relevant papers over the course of the semester. You will have 30-45 minutes to basically “teach” the paper to your classmates. This will be a commitment device for you to master the content in the paper, and it will also foster discussion. You will be expected to participate during your peers’ presentations with thoughtful questions and comments. Together this will count for one third of your grade.

The main focus of this course will be the development of a research proposal that has the potential of turning into a chapter in your dissertation (or perhaps even your job market paper). In total, this research proposal will be worth the remaining two thirds of your grade. Throughout the semester, we will discuss how it should be developing, and there will be explicit opportunities for you to get feedback on your proposal from both myself and your classmates. At the end of the semester, you will present your proposal to the class.

I don’t hold regularly scheduled office hours because I’m available pretty much anytime. You should feel free to send me an email to schedule a time to come chat for any reason. I try to be friendly, approachable, and flexible, but it is your responsibility to come talk to me.
